# Event Plan for AI LLMs 2025

## LLM Summit 2025: Shaping the Future of AI

**Event Focus:** AI LLMs in 2025
**Event Dates:** October 20-22, 2025 (3-day conference)
**Venue:** Moscone Center, San Francisco, USA
**Estimated Attendees:** 5,000 - 8,000
**Event Goal:** To convene leading researchers, developers, enterprise innovators, and policymakers to explore the cutting-edge advancements, ethical implications, and practical applications of Large Language Models, fostering collaboration and driving the responsible evolution of AI.

## 2. Logistics Plan

**A. Venue & Space Utilization (Moscone Center, San Francisco):**
*   **Main Auditorium (Moscone North/South):** Designated for all Keynote addresses, Plenary sessions, Sponsor Spotlights, and Closing remarks. Equipped with state-of-the-art audio-visual technology for high-impact presentations.
*   **Exhibition Hall A & B:**
    *   **Exhibition Hall A:** Dedicated to the Interactive Demo Zone (for startups, research projects, and new LLM applications), Research Poster Sessions, and various Networking Lounges to foster informal connections.
    *   **Exhibition Hall B:** Serves as the Centralized Registration Area, hosts all Daily Networking Breakfasts and Lunches, Coffee Breaks, and accommodates Main Sponsor Booths.
*   **Conference Rooms (Multiple Floors):**
    *   **15-20 Dedicated Breakout Rooms:** Allocated for concurrent technical sessions, policy discussions, enterprise case studies, and hands-on workshops. Capacities will range from 100-500 attendees per room to support diverse session formats.
    *   **Speaker Ready Room:** A dedicated, quiet space for speakers to prepare, rehearse, and test their presentations with technical support.
    *   **Press & Media Center:** Equipped with workstations, interview backdrops, and dedicated high-speed internet access for journalists and media personnel.
    *   **Organizers' Office & Staff Hub:** The central command center for the Eventive team and core event staff, facilitating coordination and immediate issue resolution.
    *   **Quiet Zones/Wellness Rooms:** Designated areas for attendees to take a break, relax, or engage in quiet work, promoting well-being throughout the event.
*   **Accessibility:** The Moscone Center is fully ADA compliant, featuring ramps, elevators, accessible restrooms, and designated seating areas to ensure comfort and ease of access for all attendees.

**B. Key Vendors & Responsibilities:**
*   **Eventive (Full-Service Event Management & Production):**
    *   **Overall Project Management:** Comprehensive strategic planning, meticulous budget oversight, adherence to event timelines, and seamless coordination across all vendors.
    *   **Registration Platform & Management:** Implementation of a robust online registration system, efficient on-site badge printing, and dedicated attendee support services.
    *   **Speaker & Content Management:** Management of the Call for Papers/Speakers platform, abstract review process, speaker liaison, and centralized collection of all presentation materials.
    *   **Marketing & Communications:** Development of the event website, execution of targeted email campaigns, management of the social media strategy, and coordination of PR activities.
    *   **On-Site Operations:** Coordination of all event staffing (temporary staff, volunteers), clear wayfinding signage, establishment of help desks, and development of comprehensive emergency protocols.
    *   **Event Technology:** Development and management of the event mobile app (featuring agenda, networking, Q&A, polling), and dynamic digital signage content.
    *   **Sponsorship & Exhibitor Management:** Allocation of exhibition booths, fulfillment of sponsor benefits, and serving as the primary liaison for all exhibitors.
*   **AV Concepts (Audio-Visual & Technical Production):**
    *   **Main Stage Production:** Provision of high-definition LED video walls, a powerful sound system, intelligent lighting design, stage furniture, and presenter confidence monitors for the Main Auditorium.
    *   **Breakout Room A/V:** Setup of projectors/LED screens, sound systems, microphones, and dedicated A/V technicians for each concurrent session track.
    *   **Connectivity:** Deployment of venue-wide high-speed, high-density Wi-Fi capable of supporting thousands of devices simultaneously for live demonstrations and attendee interaction, complemented by dedicated hardline internet for key presentation areas.
    *   **Live Streaming & Hybrid Capabilities:** Full technical setup for live streaming keynotes and selected sessions, with seamless integration into a virtual attendee platform.
    *   **Technical Support:** Continuous on-site A/V and IT support available throughout the duration of the event.
*   **Proof of the Pudding (Catering Services):**
    *   **Menu Design:** Creation of custom menus for breakfasts, lunches, coffee breaks, and receptions, thoughtfully designed to cater to diverse dietary needs (vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher options).
    *   **Coffee Breaks:** Morning and afternoon service featuring premium coffee, tea, pastries, and a selection of healthy snacks.
    *   **Lunch Service:** Daily options including buffet-style setups or convenient grab-and-go boxes, designed to facilitate networking and ensure timely return to sessions.
    *   **Receptions:** Innovative food and beverage stations for the Welcome Reception and Evening Networking Receptions, offering a varied culinary experience.
    *   **Gala Dinner:** Provision of a multi-course seated dinner with premium beverage service for the awards ceremony.
    *   **Sustainable Sourcing:** A commitment to prioritizing local, seasonal, and sustainably sourced ingredients where possible.

**C. Staffing & Personnel:**
*   **Eventive Core Team:** Comprised of lead event managers, a dedicated registration manager, a speaker liaison, a marketing manager, and a technical director.
*   **Temporary Staff:** Approximately 100-150 temporary staff for registration desks, ushering, session room monitoring, and exhibition hall assistance.
*   **Volunteers:** Approximately 50 local university students (from AI/CS programs) providing general assistance, wayfinding, and staffing information points.
*   **Security:** Professional security personnel managed by the Moscone Center and augmented by a third-party security firm, covering all entry points and key event areas.
*   **Medical:** An on-site first aid station staffed by paramedics for immediate medical assistance.

**D. Technology & Connectivity:**
*   **High-Density Wi-Fi:** Crucial for enabling LLM demonstrations, facilitating research presentations, and ensuring robust attendee connectivity. Redundant internet lines will be in place to prevent outages.
*   **Event Mobile App:** A central hub for the event agenda, speaker biographies, personalized schedules, venue maps, live Q&A functionalities, attendee networking features, and push notifications for important updates.
*   **Digital Signage:** Strategically placed throughout the venue for dynamic agenda updates, clear wayfinding, sponsor messages, and real-time announcements.
*   **Charging Stations:** Readily available in networking areas and lounges to keep attendees connected.
*   **Presentation Management System:** A centralized online system for speakers to upload, manage, and verify their presentations prior to their sessions.

**E. Attendee Experience:**
*   **Wayfinding:** Clear, prominent signage complemented by interactive event app maps to ensure easy navigation throughout the large venue.
*   **Comfort:** Ample seating in common areas and comfortable, ergonomic chairs in all session rooms.
*   **Networking:** Dedicated networking lounges, facilitated introductions through the event app, and structured social receptions designed to foster connections.
*   **Wellness:** Provision of quiet zones for reflection or focused work, along with readily accessible hydration stations.
*   **Sponsor & Exhibitor Engagement:** An interactive demo zone, clear pathways to exhibitor booths, and scheduled "meet the expert" sessions to maximize engagement.

**F. Sustainability Initiatives:**
*   **Digital-First Approach:** Minimizing printed materials through the use of an event app, digital signage, and electronic communications.
*   **Waste Reduction:** Implementation of comprehensive waste reduction and recycling programs, including compostable serving ware and clearly marked recycling bins.
*   **Hydration Stations:** Strategically placed water refill stations to significantly reduce single-use plastic bottle consumption.
*   **Local Sourcing:** Prioritizing locally sourced food and beverages where feasible to reduce carbon footprint.
*   **Carbon Offsetting:** Exploring carbon offsetting programs for attendee travel as an option to mitigate environmental impact.

**G. Risk Management:**
*   **Safety & Security:** A comprehensive safety and security plan developed in coordination with Moscone Center staff, private security, and local law enforcement.
*   **Emergency Response:** Clearly defined emergency response protocols covering medical incidents, fire, and evacuation procedures.
*   **Contingency Plans:** Robust contingency plans for potential A/V failures, internet outages, and speaker cancellations to ensure smooth event flow.
*   **Insurance Coverage:** Comprehensive event liability insurance and event cancellation insurance will be secured.

## 3. Estimated Costs (Budget Outline for a 5,000-8,000 attendee event)

*(Note: These are estimated ranges for a high-tier, large-scale technology conference in San Francisco. Actual costs would depend on specific negotiated contracts, final attendee numbers, and level of customization.)*

*   **1. Venue Rental (Moscone Center):**
    *   Includes main halls, multiple breakout rooms, extensive exhibition space, and basic utilities.
    *   *Estimate:* $800,000 - $1,800,000
*   **2. Audio-Visual & Production (AV Concepts):**
    *   Keynote staging, LED walls, sound systems, intelligent lighting, projection, dedicated A/V technicians for all rooms, robust high-density Wi-Fi infrastructure, internet bandwidth, and comprehensive live streaming capabilities.
    *   *Estimate:* $600,000 - $1,500,000
*   **3. Catering (Proof of the Pudding):**
    *   Covers 3x Breakfasts, 3x Lunches, 6x Coffee Breaks, 2x Networking Receptions, and 1x Gala Dinner.
    *   (Assumes an average of $150-$250 per attendee per day for food & beverage, excluding the Gala dinner.)
    *   *Estimate:* $1,500,000 - $2,500,000
*   **4. Event Management & Staffing (Eventive & Temporary Staff):**
    *   Full-service event management fee, salaries for project managers, registration staff, ushers, comprehensive security services, medical personnel, and volunteer coordination.
    *   *Estimate:* $500,000 - $1,000,000
*   **5. Marketing & Public Relations:**
    *   Development and maintenance of the event website, digital advertising campaigns, social media management, PR agency fees, and strategic media partnerships.
    *   *Estimate:* $200,000 - $400,000
*   **6. Speaker & Content Management:**
    *   Fees and travel expenses for keynote speakers, expenses for panelists, licensing for a content review platform, and abstract management software.
    *   *Estimate:* $300,000 - $700,000
*   **7. Event Technology (App, Registration Software, Digital Signage):**
    *   Development or licensing of the event mobile app, fees for the registration platform, and procurement/software for digital signage.
    *   *Estimate:* $100,000 - $250,000
*   **8. Signage & Branding:**
    *   Production of all wayfinding signs, stage backdrops, sponsor branding elements, registration desk branding, and exhibition booth signage.
    *   *Estimate:* $50,000 - $150,000
*   **9. Insurance & Legal:**
    *   Comprehensive event liability insurance and event cancellation insurance, along with legal counsel fees for contract review.
    *   *Estimate:* $30,000 - $80,000
*   **10. Contingency (10-15% of total):**
    *   An essential allocation for unforeseen expenses, last-minute changes, and potential emergencies.
    *   *Estimate:* $400,000 - $850,000

**Total Estimated Budget Range: $4,580,000 - $9,280,000**
